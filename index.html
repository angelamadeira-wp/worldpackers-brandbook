02 Logo

A traveler on top of the world.

Our symbol is a globe carrying a backpacker — a person on top of the world. It pairs with the Worldpackers wordmark in Source Sans Pro Bold. The horizontal lockup is primary; the icon stands alone where space is tight.

Lockups

Horizontal · Icon · on the brand color

Variants

One file for every background
Clear space & minimum size

Keep clear space around the logo equal to the height of the globe symbol. Minimum size: 24 px tall for the icon, 120 px wide for the horizontal lockup on screen.

✓ Do
  • Use the master files at full color, mono or reversed white.
  • Give the logo room and strong background contrast.
  • Use the icon alone for avatars, app icons and favicons.
✕ Don't
  • Recolor, add gradients, shadows or outlines.
  • Stretch, rotate, or rebuild the wordmark in another font.
  • Place it on a busy photo without the reversed version.
03 Color

Blue is who we are. Green is what to do.

A three-tier system from the Figma Foundation: primitives feed semantic tokens which adapt across light & dark. Always build with semantic tokens — never raw hex. Tap any swatch to copy it.

1 The raw colors
Primitive palette

Every color the brand owns — blue, green, red, yellow, emerald and greys — each in five shades from light to dark. Think of them as the full box of paint: the raw material, not the instructions. You almost never pick these by hand.

e.g. blue-default is the exact blue #158CBA
2 Colors with a job
Semantic colors

Each one is a role, not a shade — “text”, “action button”, “error”, “background”. Behind the scenes it points to a color from the palette, so you name what the color is for, never the hex code. This is what everyone actually uses.

e.g. cta-primary (the action button) → uses green-default

Why it works this way: change the brand blue in one place and everything that says “use the brand color” updates at once — and dark mode just works, because every role already knows its light value and its dark value. No one has to hunt down hex codes.

07 Mascot

Meet Ben, the cat.

Ben is our friendly guide — the warm, human face of the product. He welcomes, encourages, explains and celebrates: onboarding, empty states, tips and wins. He gives Worldpackers personality without ever getting in the way of the task.
